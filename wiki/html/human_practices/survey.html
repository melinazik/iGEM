Overview

Science communication to the public is truly important. It has been proposed that communicating that science exists, feeling that science is attractive, understanding that it is interesting, or being aware that science is part of one's identity are the four major objectives of public communication of science.

The four goals of science communication, as stated by the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine, are:

  1.   sharing recent findings and excitement for science
  2.   increasing public appreciation of science
  3.   increasing knowledge and understanding of science
  4.   influencing the opinions, policy preferences or behavior of people
  5.   ensuring that a diversity of perspectives about science held by different groups are considered when solutions to societal problems are pursued

Having all these in mind, and more specifically goals (3), (4), and (5), we decided to create a questionnaire to assess the public's understanding of key biological concepts, and to gather views on general policy issues, a major field of synthetic biology. The available answers to all questions were Strongly Disagree, Disagree, Don’t Know, Agree, Strongly Agree.

Our survey was approved by the Research Ethics and Conduct Committee of the Democritus University of Thrace.


The section about general understanding of biological concepts consisted of the following questions:

  •    DNA contains information about the structure and function of the organism to which it belongs.
  •    RNA is synthesized based on DNA.
  •    Plants are living organisms.
  •    RNA can modify DNA.
  •    All organisms on Earth have a common ancestor.
  •    Some antibiotics are effective against viruses.
  •    Human somatic and gamete (eggs, sperm) cells have the same DNA.
  •    Mushrooms are plants.
  •    Genetically modified organisms (GMOs) are used to make drugs.
  •    DNA fragments of genetically modified organisms we consume can modify or become integrated into the DNA of human cells./li>
  •    The use of stem cells for research or therapeutic purposes requires the killing of embryos.

The second section of the survey contained questions on the general public’s opinion on policy issues of synthetic biology. The questions were:

  •    A genetically modified organism or cell is any organism or cell whose genetic material has been modified using genetic engineering techniques. I understand this definition.
  •    Genetic modification of organisms or cells is, by definition, immoral.
  •    Consumption of any genetically modified organism (GMO) is proven to be dangerous to health.
  •    The use of GMOs for drug production is a beneficial practice.
  •    Genetic modification of human somatic cells for the treatment of diseases could be beneficial (cell changes are not inherited in the offspring).
  •    Genetic modification of human somatic cells for the treatment of diseases is dangerous and should not be investigated.

The collection of the public’s answers happened through our Social Media pages as well as during our presence in the 85th International Thessaloniki Fair (HELEXPO) where we were able to collect 277 responses.

Once the survey was completed and the results were analyzed, we created a presentation covering the first section of the questionnaire, explaining the correct answer to each question. This presentation was showcased and discussed with the public during our initiative called “Lab on the Street” that took place on the 9th of October 2021.
