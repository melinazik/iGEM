29/11 - 05/12

November 2020

After long research, reading papers on different subjects and various projects from the past years we concluded that our project would concern Osteoarthritis and in particular we would propose a new approach for the treatment of this musculoskeletal disease. You can find more on what our idea included in the beginning and ow it all started on the following link (link για description)

We made our final decision about our project on December 3rd.

06/12 - 12/12

December 2020

After we concluded on the project we studied more specific things about Osteoarthritis in pathophysiological and molecular level so that we had a clearer idea on what is happening in a joint when a patient suffers from Osteoarthritis. We tried to answer the following questions: How it begins? What causes it? Which molecules and cell types play an important role in an Osteoarthritic joint? Which are the existing therapies for this disease? What should happen in order to stop the progression of the disease.

Trying to answer these questions we had the need as a team to have a meeting with someone who has experience with this disease and is a professional Orthopaedic Surgeon. For that purpose, we prepared a presentation about our team, the competition and our idea as it was in the beginning and contacted Dr. Georgios I. Drosos (Orthopaedic Surgeon and a Chairman at the University Department of Orthopaedic Surgery and a Professor of Orthopaedics in the School of Medicine, Democritus University of Thrace). Thankfully Dr. Drosos was very interested in our project which led to his recruitment in our team on 10/12 as a PI to help us throughout the duration of the competition.

First iGEM Stuff Call : December 11th

Also, on 11/12 we held our first igem stuff call to have a session discussing all the things concerning the iGEM competition. We held a weekly “iGEM stuff” call every week after that to discuss all terms and conditions of the competition, the deadlines, the medal criteria, the awards, the registry and the safety form, in order to make sure that the whole team has a clear idea of what we need to do in the competition in order to succeed.

13/12 - 16/01

As it is expected every team in a competition of these standards as the iGEM, needs a financial support, so in this week we had to compose our project proposal containing a meticulous description of the competition, of all the members of the team, including the Principal Investigators, a very descriptive analysis of our project concerning both the wet lab and the dry lab and last but not least the budget that we were expecting to need for the implementation of our project.

At the same time, a pitching presentation was prepared containing a short description of the competition, our team, our project and our needs in order to present when holding a meeting a candidate sponsor. On 16/01 the first version of our proposal and pitching presentation was formed.

Our wet lab team was focused on understanding every aspect of the pathophysiology of Osteoarthritis at a molecular level. We started our first efforts to select which cell type is the most suitable for our approach and which factors should be expressed on our genetic circuit forming a primitive design of our experiments and writing it down on the project proposal. (for more information on what was the thinking process in order to conclude on the experiments we run you can click on the following link (link gia engineering).

17/01 - 30/01

January 2021

On 17/12 we contacted the first companies which we were hoping to be our sponsors while the project proposal was being daily updated with additions of every aspect.

The wet lab team was perfecting the text on the project proposal concerning the Osteoarthritis and the Wet Lab experiments. We also formed a list containing the most promising factors to be included in our genetic circuit, where we added new things as soon as we learned about them. We aimed to find 3-4 factors in total, which would affect the anabolic and the catabolic pathways in the articular cartilage and the pain that the patient is experiencing during Osteoarthritis.

31/01 - 22/02

We took a break for the semester exams of our departments.

22/02 - 28/02

February 2021

The Wet team Lab started analyzing the sequences of our genetic circuit and choosing the vectors in which our genetic would be inserted. We held our call on 25/02 to discuss our ideas.

We started studying in order to choose whether the transfection of the cells in our approach would be transient or stable. We considered all the advantages and disadvantages of each method both for our approach as a treatment and the feasibility of it to implement it in the Lab. We held our call on 28/02 to discuss our ideas.

01/03 - 07/03

March 2021

The Wet Lab of our team started reconsidering the genetic circuit and exploring other alternatives based on RNA circuits. We studied papers considering the RNA circuits and tried to design an RNA circuit that would be suitable for our approach. We held a meeting on 7/03 to discuss our new findings.

08/03 - 14/03

The wet lab studied ways for measuring the factors expressed by the genetic circuit and design of the experiments. Research and study in order to find the protocols and kits most suitable for our approach. We held a meeting to discuss our new findings on 14/03.

Another concern that we had to resolve was what experiments we should run in order to be sure that our circuit produced the expected factors and would eventually have effects in an osteoarthritic joint. We tried to find a way to simulate the Osteoarthritic environment in vitro or ex vivo while excluding the possibility of running in vivo experiments due to the restrictions of both time and the competition. We held our meeting on 21/03 to talk about the new things that came up.

In parallel, our team started thinking and researching what needed to be done in order to open a crowdfunding page.

22/03 - 28/03

Our Wet Lab team had done a lot of research. We were in very good level of knowledge regarding Osteoarthritis at all levels, and specifically at a molecular level. But we had many alternatives for our project, including the protocols that we would have to use if we concluded in any of them. Therefore, we needed the opinion of an experienced one, working on Osteoarthritis at a molecular level to help us decide which were the most promising and at the same time most feasible of our alternatives.

For that purpose, we worked in this week to prepare a document which meticulously described our idea and the steps that we were planning to make in the laboratory (experiments and protocols). We contacted, the one to later become one of our Principal Investigator, Mrs. Maria Margy Koffa. Mrs. Koffa is a professor on the department of Molecular Biology & Genetics, head of the Cellular Biology Laboratory and has had previous experience on Osteoarthritis.

Also, our team concluded on what needed to be done in order to open a crowdfunding page and from point and on (25/03) we started designing our crowdfunding page.

While posting various scientific related posts on our social media throughout this whole time, our project reveal started on 27/03. We prepared a total of 7 posts dedicated to our project with many hints and riddles for our followers to find our project idea.
